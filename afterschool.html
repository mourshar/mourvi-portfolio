Project Overview

An afterschool STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) program designed for children aged 6-8 years. This program brought engaging, hands-on science and math activities to students outside of regular school hours, using the beloved Galli Galli Sim Sim characters to make learning exciting and accessible.

My Responsibilities

I held three key roles in establishing this initiative:

Concept and Curriculum Design

  • Developed the overall program concept and learning framework
  • Created age-appropriate STEM curriculum aligned with educational standards
  • Designed activities that made complex concepts accessible to 6-8 year olds
  • Integrated Sesame Street characters and storytelling to enhance engagement

Lesson Plan Development

  • Created detailed lesson plans for each session
  • Designed hands-on activities and experiments
  • Developed materials and resource lists for educators
  • Established learning objectives and assessment criteria

Teacher Hiring and Training

  • Developed criteria and conducted hiring process for program instructors
  • Created comprehensive training program for teachers
  • Conducted training sessions on curriculum delivery and Sesame Workshop methodology
  • Provided ongoing support and professional development

Program Focus Areas

The afterschool program covered essential STEM topics through hands-on, exploratory learning:

Science

  • Basic scientific method and inquiry skills
  • Simple experiments and observations
  • Nature and environment exploration
  • States of matter and simple chemistry

Technology

  • Introduction to tools and how they work
  • Simple machines and mechanisms
  • Age-appropriate technology concepts

Engineering

  • Design thinking for young learners
  • Building and construction activities
  • Problem-solving through making

Mathematics

  • Patterns and relationships
  • Measurement and data collection
  • Spatial reasoning
  • Applied math in real-world contexts

Design Principles

The program was built on key principles for effective STEM education with young children:

  • Hands-On Learning: Children learned by doing, experimenting, and exploring rather than through passive instruction
  • Inquiry-Based: Encouraged children to ask questions, make predictions, and discover answers through investigation
  • Developmentally Appropriate: All activities were designed specifically for the 6-8 age range, matching their cognitive and physical capabilities
  • Engaging Characters: Integrated beloved Sesame Street characters to maintain interest and create familiar entry points into new concepts
  • Teacher Support: Provided comprehensive resources and training to ensure teachers felt confident delivering STEM content

Program Impact

By establishing both the educational framework and the instructor team, this program successfully:

  • Introduced STEM concepts to children who might not otherwise have access to such learning
  • Built confidence in both students and teachers around science and math
  • Created a replicable model for afterschool STEM programming
  • Demonstrated that young children can engage meaningfully with complex concepts when properly scaffolded

Critical Role

My involvement was critical to the program's establishment and success:

  • Set the foundational vision and educational approach
  • Created all instructional materials and lesson plans
  • Built the teaching team from the ground up
  • Ensured alignment with Sesame Workshop's educational standards and research-based practices
